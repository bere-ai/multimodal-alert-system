Multimodal Alert System

A real-time monitoring demo that watches oxygen and temperature levels and delivers synchronized visual, vibration, and message alerts with clear severity-based escalation.

Problem

Critical conditions are easy to miss when the response is fragmented.

Monitoring environments need more than raw sensor values. Operators need a system that makes risk visible, obvious, and immediately actionable.

Single-channel alerts get lost

A plain text warning or a single flashing light can be overlooked when attention is split across multiple tasks.

Severity must be instantly readable

Teams need a clear difference between mild changes, rising concern, and truly urgent states without having to interpret a chart first.

Escalation should stay consistent

The alert experience should respond the same way every time so a demo, a classroom submission, or a prototype all feel intentional.

How It Works

A straightforward rules engine drives a coordinated alert response.

01

Read the inputs

The system tracks oxygen level and temperature as the monitored signals in the demo loop.

02

Evaluate thresholds

Each signal is checked against fixed severity ranges, and the highest priority alert becomes the active system response.

03

Trigger multimodal output

The product emits a visual cue, a vibration pattern, and a short message so urgency is obvious even in a fast-moving demo.

Alert Levels

Three alert levels plus a normal baseline keep the response simple and readable.

Advisory

Blue light

Light vibration

Early condition change detected (e.g., oxygen decreasing or temperature rising).

Example: “Oxygen level slightly decreasing.”

Warning

Yellow flash

Medium vibration

Oxygen at 50% or below, or temperature at 100°F or above.

Example: “Temperature rising.”

Critical

Red flash

Strong vibration

Oxygen at 25% or below, or temperature at 95°F or above.

Example: “Oxygen critically low.”
